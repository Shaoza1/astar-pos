## CONTEXT — WHAT THIS POS SYSTEM DOES

Astar POS is a hospitality point-of-sale system built for a restaurant/bar (Die Blikkasteel) inside a guest house. The core problems it solves:

- **Recipe-level stock deduction** — when a &quot;Butlers Breakfast&quot; is sold, the system automatically deducts 2 bacon rashers, 2 eggs, 2 bread slices, and 1 tomato from stock
- **Unit conversion** — stock is purchased in one unit (loaf of bread) but consumed in another (slices). 1 loaf = 20 slices must be tracked accurately
- **Delivery reconciliation** — what was ordered vs. what was actually received, with discrepancy flagging
- **Biometric + PIN staff authentication** — replacing a shared 4-digit PIN system that enables fraud
- **Full audit trail** — every price change, void, discount, and stock adjustment logged permanently
- **Variance reporting** — expected stock vs. actual count, filterable by shortages only / overs only

## CONTEXT — WHY THIS MODULE EXISTS

Die Blikkasteel&#39;s current POS tracks items sold but never deducts real ingredients from stock. A &quot;Butlers Breakfast&quot; sale records R75 revenue but does not deduct 2 bacon rashers, 2 eggs, 2 bread slices, or 1 tomato. This is the root cause of stock that never balances.

The ingredient engine solves this by:
1. Storing every raw ingredient with its purchase unit and consumption unit
2. Converting between units automatically (1 loaf = 20 slices, 1 bottle = 30 measures)
3. Tracking current stock at the consumption unit level
4. Alerting when stock falls below a configurable threshold
5. Logging every stock movement permanently for audit
